# Prior Review Note - CECL Allowance (Reference for Q1 2026)

## Context
The prior quarterly review emphasized consistency between CECL documentation, reserve engine configuration, and reported results, with a focus on scenario ordering and the transparency of qualitative adjustments.

## Items carried forward
1. **Horizon alignment risk**
   - Prior review requested confirmation that the reasonable and supportable forecast horizon and reversion settings used in production match the CECL policy documentation.
   - Follow-up expectation: evidence of configuration control, documented approvals for deviations, and quantified impact assessment.

2. **Scenario ordering reasonableness checks**
   - Prior review recommended formalized monitoring to ensure **Severe** results are generally more conservative than **Adverse** at segment level unless a documented scenario-path rationale exists.

3. **Overlay transparency**
   - Prior review noted that the narrative in the overlay memorandum should clearly communicate:
     - the basis of the overlay (bps applied to what metric),
     - whether overlay values are constrained by a cap, and
     - the consolidated impact.

## Status entering Q1 2026 review
- Horizon alignment and scenario ordering remain priority review topics given the current scenario designs and the observed sensitivity of Residential Mortgage losses to house price paths.
- Overlay disclosure is expected to include clear linkage between policy limits (cap) and the values applied.
