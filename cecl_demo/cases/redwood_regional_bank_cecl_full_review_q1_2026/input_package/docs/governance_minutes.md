# Governance Minutes - ACL / CECL Committee (Q1 2026 Close)

**Committee:** ACL / CECL Governance Committee  
**Institution:** Redwood Regional Bank  
**Meeting date:** Q1 2026 close window  
**Subject:** Q1 2026 CECL Allowance Review - quantitative results, overlays, and key model risk items

## 1. Attendees
- Chief Credit Officer (Chair)
- Controller
- Head of Credit Risk Analytics (Model Owner)
- Allowance / CECL Program Manager
- Model Risk Management (MRM) Representative
- Commercial Credit Administration Representative
- Retail Credit Risk Representative
- Internal Audit (observer)

## 2. Agenda
1. Review macroeconomic scenarios (Baseline/Adverse/Severe) and key movements
2. Review segment quantitative allowance results and key drivers
3. Review qualitative overlays and rationale
4. Model risk topics and required follow-ups
5. Approvals and action items

## 3. Scenario review (discussion highlights)
- Credit Risk Analytics summarized scenario paths through 2027Q4.
- Committee noted that Severe includes a sharper shock in 2026 followed by partial recovery, including a more favorable house price growth profile after 2026Q4 relative to Adverse.
- MRM requested that scenario ordering expectations be assessed at the segment level where macro paths are non-monotonic (e.g., collateral recovery).

## 4. Quantitative results review (segment-level observations)
- CRE: Results increase appropriately from Baseline to Adverse and Severe; committee agreed sensitivity to CRE price growth is consistent with portfolio risk.
- C&I: Results responsive to GDP and rate path; committee requested additional commentary on industries driving dispersion.
- Residential Mortgage: Committee discussed that Severe results appeared **directionally unusual** relative to Adverse based on the summary presented.
  - Model Owner indicated the Severe house price recovery and overlay posture may both contribute.
  - MRM requested a decomposition to separate macro impacts from overlay impacts.
- Consumer Unsecured: Committee agreed higher sensitivity to unemployment is reasonable; requested continued monitoring of utilization and early-stage delinquency.

## 5. Overlay discussion
- Allowance Program Manager reviewed overlay schedule by segment and scenario.
- Committee discussed overlay governance guardrails and referenced the **documented overlay cap of 5.0 bps**.
- MRM observed that Severe overlays for CRE, C&I, and Consumer Unsecured exceed the documented cap and requested formal exception documentation and approval rationale.
- Committee noted the Severe mortgage overlay is negative and requested additional support for why relief is appropriate under a stress scenario, particularly given consumer affordability and rate sensitivity.

## 6. Model risk items (MRM points raised)
1. **Horizon/reversion alignment:** MRM reiterated the need to confirm that the reserve engine aligns to the **documented 4-quarter forecast and 4-quarter linear reversion** assumption.
2. **Residential Mortgage directionality:** MRM flagged the Severe vs. Adverse ordering as a reasonableness concern requiring documented explanation.
3. **Overlay transparency:** MRM requested enhanced disclosure of exposure-weighted overlay impact and reconciliation to cap language.

## 7. Decisions
- Committee approved submission of the Q1 2026 allowance recommendation contingent upon completion of post-close documentation items listed below.
- Committee agreed that any overlay cap exceptions must be explicitly documented and retained with the quarter close package.
