# Qualitative Overlay Memorandum - Q1 2026 CECL Allowance Review

## Purpose
This memorandum documents qualitative adjustments ("overlays") applied in conjunction with the quantitative CECL estimate for Redwood Regional Bank's **Q1 2026 CECL Allowance Review**. Overlays are intended to address known model limitations, emerging risks, and factors not fully captured in the historical calibration.

## Summary of overlay posture
Overlay usage this quarter is intended to be **targeted and modest**, with adjustments applied in basis points at the segment level by scenario. The overlay schedule is designed to fine-tune the quantitative result rather than materially reshape the overall allowance conclusion.

A documented governance threshold exists for overlays:
- **Documented overlay cap:** **5.0 bps**

Management considers the overlay levels to be within normal operating practice and consistent with prior-quarter methodology, with the primary purpose of maintaining conservative posture where uncertainty remains.

## Overlay schedule (bps) by segment and scenario
### Baseline
- CRE: **+2 bps**
- C&I: **+2 bps**
- Residential Mortgage: **+1 bp**
- Consumer Unsecured: **+3 bps**

### Adverse
- CRE: **+5 bps**
- C&I: **+4 bps**
- Residential Mortgage: **+2 bps**
- Consumer Unsecured: **+6 bps**

### Severe
- CRE: **+8 bps**
- C&I: **+7 bps**
- Residential Mortgage: **-9 bps**
- Consumer Unsecured: **+9 bps**

## Rationale by segment
### Commercial Real Estate (CRE)
- **Rationale:** CRE remains sensitive to vacancy/refinance risk and collateral value volatility. The adverse and severe overlays reflect caution around refinancing conditions and the pace at which CRE values normalize.
- **Notes:** The severe overlay reflects heightened downside risk recognition but is expected to be applied consistently with governance expectations.

### Commercial & Industrial (C&I)
- **Rationale:** C&I default dynamics remain sensitive to unemployment and GDP growth. Overlays provide incremental conservatism for borrower performance dispersion and lagged default recognition.

### Residential Mortgage
- **Rationale:** Mortgage performance remains supported by borrower equity and underwriting quality, and the model captures substantial collateral sensitivity through house price growth. In severe conditions, management considered that certain relief/mitigant effects (e.g., seasoning, loss mitigation effectiveness, and collateral recovery dynamics) may partially offset modeled stress.
- **Implementation note:** A negative overlay is applied under the severe scenario to reflect these mitigating considerations.

### Consumer Unsecured
- **Rationale:** Consumer unsecured is highly sensitive to labor market conditions and payment shock. Overlays recognize uncertainty around borrower cash-flow resilience and utilization behavior in stress.

## Overall conclusion
Management views the overlay set as a practical refinement to the quantitative estimate. While overlays vary by scenario and segment, the intent is to keep the overall effect **within a controlled range** and to maintain comparability quarter over quarter.

## Items for ongoing monitoring
- Validate that overlay directions remain consistent with scenario narratives and portfolio performance.
- Reassess whether the overlay framework appropriately reflects the documented cap expectations.
- Continue monitoring mortgage scenario ordering and the interaction between house price paths and the severe mortgage overlay.
