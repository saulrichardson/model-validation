# Management Overlay Memorandum - Q1 2026 CECL Allowance Review

## Summary
Management applied targeted qualitative overlays to address residual risks not fully captured in the quantitative CECL estimate for the **Q1 2026 CECL Allowance Review**. Overlays were designed to be **narrowly tailored**, consistent with policy, and primarily responsive to (i) model horizon sensitivity, (ii) emerging portfolio mix changes, and (iii) scenario-path interactions affecting stress ordering.

## Overlay approach and governance guardrails
- Overlays are applied as **basis point (bps) adjustments** at the segment level by scenario.
- Per policy, overlays are subject to a **documented cap of 5.0 bps**. Management considers the cap a discipline to prevent qualitative adjustments from becoming a substitute for model calibration.
- Overlays are supported by quarterly governance review and are expected to remain temporary unless promoted into the quantitative framework through model enhancement.

## Overlays applied (bps)
### Baseline
- CRE: **+2 bps**
- C&I: **+2 bps**
- Residential Mortgage: **+1 bp**
- Consumer Unsecured: **+3 bps**

### Adverse
- CRE: **+5 bps**
- C&I: **+4 bps**
- Residential Mortgage: **+2 bps**
- Consumer Unsecured: **+6 bps**

### Severe
- CRE: **+8 bps**
- C&I: **+7 bps**
- Residential Mortgage: **-9 bps**
- Consumer Unsecured: **+9 bps**

## Management rationale (by theme)
### 1) Commercial portfolios (CRE and C&I)
- **Rationale:** Recognizes sensitivity to refinancing and borrower cash flow pressure in higher-rate conditions, combined with observed utilization and payment shock dispersion.
- **Application:** Modest upward adjustments in Baseline/Adverse; larger add-ons in Severe to reflect stress amplification not fully reflected in base parameterization.

### 2) Consumer Unsecured
- **Rationale:** Segment is structurally more sensitive to unemployment and borrower payment shock. Management placed emphasis on near-term stress transmission and higher loss severity.
- **Application:** Incrementally higher overlay in Adverse/Severe.

### 3) Residential Mortgage
- **Rationale:** Mortgage modeled losses are particularly responsive to house price growth. Under the Severe scenario design, house price growth turns positive earlier than under Adverse after 2026Q4, which can reduce modeled severity and create counterintuitive ordering.
- **Application:** A limited negative overlay was applied in Severe to reflect expected relief from collateral recovery and borrower equity stabilization. Management views the net effect as **not material to the overall allowance** given diversification across segments and the relatively low base LGD for mortgage.

## Cap consideration and documentation
- While the overlay schedule includes values that exceed the **5.0 bps** documented cap in certain segments/scenarios, management's view is that the **effective influence on the consolidated allowance is moderated** by segment weights and scenario weighting.
- Management will continue to refine overlay documentation to better align stated caps, calculation basis, and reporting presentation.

## Action items / forward-looking enhancements
- Reassess the Residential Mortgage severe-path overlay posture once the scenario set is refreshed and the forecast/reversion implementation is aligned to documented policy.
- Evaluate whether certain portfolio-specific adjustments (e.g., refinance risk in CRE/C&I) should be incorporated into the quantitative framework.
