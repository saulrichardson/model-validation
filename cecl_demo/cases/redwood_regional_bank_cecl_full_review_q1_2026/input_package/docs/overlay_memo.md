# Qualitative Overlay Memo - Q1 2026 CECL Allowance Review

**Bank:** Redwood Regional Bank  
**Portfolio:** Q1 2026 CECL Allowance Review  
**Date:** Q1 2026 close  

## 1. Executive summary
Management applied targeted qualitative overlays to address near-term uncertainty and known model limitations not fully captured in the quantitative reserve engine. Overlays were designed to be **modest** and focused on segments where emerging performance and forward-looking indicators suggest incremental risk.

While overlays were applied across scenarios, the intent was not to substitute for the scenario framework but to refine results for items where internal monitoring identified potential gaps (e.g., borrower sensitivity to sustained higher rates, localized stress in certain CRE submarkets, and unsecured consumer budget pressure).

## 2. Overlay posture and governance guardrails
- Overlays are applied as **bps of exposure** at the segment/scenario level.
- Management referenced the **documented overlay cap of 5.0 bps** as a guiding control to support disciplined use and consistency.
- Overlay recommendations were discussed with Finance, Credit Risk, and Model Risk prior to committee submission.

## 3. Applied overlay schedule (bps of exposure)
### 3.1 Baseline overlays
- CRE: **+2.0 bps**
- C&I: **+2.0 bps**
- Residential Mortgage: **+1.0 bps**
- Consumer Unsecured: **+3.0 bps**

### 3.2 Adverse overlays
- CRE: **+5.0 bps**
- C&I: **+4.0 bps**
- Residential Mortgage: **+2.0 bps**
- Consumer Unsecured: **+6.0 bps**

### 3.3 Severe overlays
- CRE: **+8.0 bps**
- C&I: **+7.0 bps**
- Residential Mortgage: **-9.0 bps**
- Consumer Unsecured: **+9.0 bps**

## 4. Rationale by segment
### 4.1 Commercial Real Estate (CRE)
**Rationale:** Increased sensitivity to refinancing conditions and capitalization rate pressure remains a key theme. While current delinquency remains contained, watchlist activity and appraisal refresh outcomes warrant incremental conservatism outside baseline.

**Implementation note:** Overlays are scaled upward under Adverse/Severe to reflect potential price and liquidity shock transmission.

### 4.2 Commercial & Industrial (C&I)
**Rationale:** Higher-for-longer rates and margin compression remain key vulnerabilities for select leveraged borrowers. Management noted increased dispersion in quarterly covenant compliance and reduced earnings stability in certain industries.

**Implementation note:** Overlays under Adverse/Severe were intended to be incremental and consistent with observed risk-rating migration.

### 4.3 Residential Mortgage
**Rationale:** The mortgage portfolio continues to benefit from seasoning, borrower equity, and strong credit scores. Given the Severe macro path's improvement in house price growth beginning 2027Q1, management viewed the need for incremental mortgage conservatism under Severe as limited.

**Implementation note:** A mortgage overlay relief posture under Severe was retained to avoid double-counting collateral recovery already reflected in macro paths. Management views the net impact as limited in overall ACL due to the segment's strong credit profile.

### 4.4 Consumer Unsecured
**Rationale:** Unsecured borrowers remain most exposed to inflationary budget stress and employment volatility. Internal collections metrics and utilization trends support additional conservatism, particularly under Adverse/Severe.

**Implementation note:** Overlays were applied to capture tail risk not fully reflected in recent benign realized loss experience.

## 5. Key considerations and limitations
- Overlays are intended to be temporary and should be revisited quarterly based on performance, risk-rating migration, and updated scenario information.
- Overlay effects may not be strictly monotonic by scenario at the segment level when management believes macro recoveries (e.g., collateral price rebound) warrant relief.
- Management will continue monitoring scenario results for reasonableness and will refine overlay documentation where needed to improve transparency.

## 6. Conclusion
Management believes the overlay approach reasonably addresses near-term uncertainties and targeted model limitations while maintaining alignment with the broader scenario framework. The overlay schedule will be reassessed in Q2 2026 as additional performance data and refreshed economic outlook become available.
